# Vertical snippets

Slot these into the `{{Hook}}` and `{{Example}}` fields of any template, picked by
the row's **Type** column. The Hook names a pain they actually feel; the Example
is a concrete automation. Both are written to be *true in general* — they don't
claim anything specific about that business you can't back up.

> **Optional, higher-converting opener (Tier A/B only):** when the row has real
> review data, lead with it — it's specific and verifiable:
> *"You've clearly built something people trust — {{Reviews}} reviews at {{Rating}} stars caught my eye."*

> **Finance verticals only — lead with your background (Accounting, Financial advisor,
> Insurance, Mortgage):** here your résumé *is* the hook — it says "I speak your
> language," not "look at me." Open with it instead of the generic intro:
> *"I'm Chauncey — I spent years in accounting and business intelligence at Google,
> American Express, and KPMG, so I know how much of your week disappears into
> reporting, reconciliations, and data entry. That's exactly what I now automate for
> firms like {{Business}} here in West LA."*
> For every other vertical, skip the background and keep the standard opener — there
> it's noise.
